Nasi Goreng

Indonesian fried rice dish bursting with flavors from stir-fried rice, vegetables, protein, and aromatic spices

Nasi Goreng is a beloved Indonesian dish, consisting of stir-fried rice with a mix of vegetables, protein, and aromatic spices, creating a delightful medley of flavors and textures. It is a true representation of the rich and diverse tastes found in Indonesian cuisine.

Each mouthful offers an explosion of tastes, making it an absolute delight for both locals and visitors. Beyond its culinary appeal, Nasi Goreng holds a deeper cultural significance, symbolizing togetherness and hospitality in Indonesia, making it more than just a meal but a cherished tradition that brings people closer.

Ingredients

  • Cooking Spray
  • 3 large eggs, beaten
  • 1 tbs vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 leek, chopped
  • 2 green chile peppers, chopped
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1/2 pound skinless, boneless chicken breasts, cut into thin strips
  • 1/2 pound peeled and deveined shrimp
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 3 cups cold cooked white rice
  • 3 tbs kecap manis (sweet soy sauce)

Steps

  1. Heat a large nonstick skillet over medium heat. Coat with nonstick cooking spray.
  2. Pour eggs into the hot skillet and cook until the edges begin to set. Lift the edges to allow any uncooked egg to flow onto the hot pan, about 1 minute. Flip omelet in one piece and cook until fully set, about 30 seconds. Transfer omelet to a cutting board and slice into 1/2-inch strips. Set aside.
  3. Heat vegetable oil in a wok or large frying pan over high heat. Stir in onion, leek, chile peppers, and garlic. Cook and stir until onion is soft, 3 to 5 minutes. Stir in chicken, shrimp, coriander, and cumin; mix well. Cook and stir for about 5 minutes.
  4. Mix in cooked rice, sweet soy sauce, and omelet strips; cook until rice is heated through and chicken is no longer pink in the center, 3 to 5 minutes.
